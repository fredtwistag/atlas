Three observations worth your time

1. The Operations team is high-trust, low-tool. Contributors were unusually candid about workarounds and where the standard process breaks. The team's biggest asset — informal coordination — is also its biggest constraint when scaling. Most of the highest-impact opportunities cluster around making this informal coordination explicit, not replacing it.

2. One person is the single point of three bottlenecks. VP Sales appears as the gating decision-maker in 3 of the top 5 opportunities. He's aware. He told us directly he'd save 6 hours a week with rule-based pre-approval. Fixing this one node unlocks downstream wins.

3. Foundation gaps will block one opportunity. "AI-assisted quote drafting" is technically feasible but depends on cleaning ~2 years of historical quote data first. We've flagged this as a 4-week foundation project that should precede the build.

02 · Methodology

How we got here.

4 sessions per contributor, 4 weeks, conversational. Each team member had four 4-6 minute conversations with Atlas over the course of the sprint, covering quote-to-cash flow, exception handling, day-to-day tooling, and one open prompt.

Three sources of signal. Each opportunity is grounded in: (1) verbatim quotes from contributor conversations, attributed by role, (2) corroborating system signals from Salesforce, NetSuite, and calendar metadata, and (3) when relevant, comparable cases from Twistag's portfolio with verified outcomes.

Scoring is multi-dimensional and auditable. Every opportunity has a composite score across financial impact, time-to-ship, AI-suitability, change management cost, and dependency depth. Every score has visible rationale you can click into.

What this report is. A decision document. The opportunities ranked here are recommendations, not directives. Approve, defer, or remove anything — Atlas tracks the choices made and learns from them.

What this report isn't. An evaluation of any individual. Atlas does not score contributors. The 47 conversations behind this report fed an aggregate map of work — never a record of person-by-person performance.

Automate enterprise pricing pre-approval workflow

AI-assisted quote drafting from past similar deals

$120K – $180K / yr · High confidence · 9 evidence points · 4-6 weeks · foundation work first

AEs manually assemble quotes from SKU sheets. No enterprise template exists; pattern-matching to similar past deals lives in two sales reps' heads — one retiring in 8 months. 04 · Foundation gaps

What to fix before
you build on top.

Two foundational issues showed up that need attention before certain opportunities can ship cleanly. These aren't AI projects — they're plumbing.

Historical quote data lives in 11 different files
Past enterprise quote outcomes are scattered across SharePoint, AE laptops, and Salesforce attachments. Opportunity #2 (AI-assisted quote drafting) cannot ship without ~3 weeks of cleanup. Twistag can scope as a foundation engagement, ~$28K, but this is a one-time investment.
No single source of truth for "who can approve what"
Approval matrix is informal — comes up in conversations as "ask João, he'll know" or "check with Rita first." This blocks the auto-routing logic in Opportunity #1. We recommend a 2-week alignment workshop with VP Sales, Finance, and Ops to formalize the matrix before the build starts.

What we noticed but didn't flag

Several conversations referenced legacy systems (in particular, the 2018 ERP that nobody loves). We did not flag this as a foundation issue because none of the top 12 opportunities depend on replacing it. Atlas can sit on top of the legacy ERP for everything in this report — no rip-and-replace needed.

Methodological transparency

How impact ranges are estimated. Each opportunity uses three inputs: contributor-reported frequency, system data on prevalence, and Twistag's comparable cases. Estimates are conservative ranges with confidence bands — never single point estimates.

How quotes are attributed. By role and session number. Names are never attached to quotes in this report. Atlas internal records can map quotes back to contributors only with their consent for opportunity-level evidence.

What we changed since Atlas v0.5. Scoring rubric updated based on outcomes from prior sprints. Higher weight on dependency depth than before — this report would have ranked #2 ahead of #1 under the old rubric.
