How it works

From map to ship, in 4 weeks.

A repeatable three-phase rhythm. Same process every time. Different outcome every time.

Phase 1

Capture

The manager invites a team. Each person does 4 sessions over 3-4 weeks — 20-25 minutes total of their time. Atlas asks open questions about workflow, exceptions, frustrations, and tools.

→Day 1 — sprint launched, invites sent
→Day 7 — first sessions complete
→Day 14 — patterns emerging
→Day 21 — top opportunities ranked
Phase 2

Surface

Atlas builds an operational knowledge graph from the conversations. Each bottleneck, decision dependency, and workaround becomes a node. The scoring engine ranks opportunities by impact, time-to-ship, and confidence.

→40+ conversations → 150+ quotes
→Captured patterns across the team
→5-10 opportunities ranked
→1-3 high-impact · 2-4 quick wins
Phase 3

Ship

The sponsor reviews each opportunity with full evidence. Approve one — you get a pre-drafted SOW ready for delivery. Either via Twistag's FDE team or your own engineers. Either way: code, not slides.

→Click "Approve for FDE"
→SOW drafted in 30 seconds
→Scope alignment in 48 hours
→First ship in 2-4 weeks
Why not what already exists

Atlas isn't another search bar or another deck.

Enterprise search (Glean, Microsoft Copilot)

Retrieval-first. Finds what's written down. The bottleneck is — most of what matters isn't written down anywhere.

vs

Atlas

Outbound-first. Asks the right questions to capture what's only in people's heads. Then makes it queryable.

Traditional consulting (MBB, Big 4)

$250K-$1.5M, 3-6 months. Excellent diagnostics. Slide deck deliverable. Lives in someone's drawer 90 days later.

vs

Atlas

$25K-$95K, 3-4 weeks. Conversational diagnostics + persistent product + pre-drafted shippable work. Backlog that compounds.

Process mining (Celonis, UiPath)

Sees what your systems do. Misses what people do outside the systems — decisions, exceptions, workarounds in spreadsheets.

vs

Atlas

Sees both. Conversation captures the human layer. System connectors capture the data layer. Both feed the same operational map.
